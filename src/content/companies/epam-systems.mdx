# Is EPAM Systems the right AI agent development company for you?

EPAM Systems is a strong choice for large enterprises that need AI agent development delivered with the scale, governance, and compliance rigour that a 50,000+ engineer organisation can provide. It is not suited to startups or mid-market companies seeking a boutique, AI-first partner with fast time-to-first-prototype — EPAM's process overhead and enterprise pricing reflect its size and client base.

## What services does EPAM Systems offer for AI agent development?

EPAM's AI practice sits within its broader technology consulting and engineering organisation. It covers AI agent design, LLM orchestration, MLOps infrastructure, enterprise system integration, and large-scale consulting engagements. EPAM serves clients across financial services, insurance, media, healthcare, and manufacturing.

**Core service areas:**
- **Custom AI agents and LLM orchestration:** Design and deployment of agentic systems using LangChain, OpenAI APIs, and Anthropic APIs within enterprise technology stacks.
- **MLOps and AI infrastructure:** Model deployment pipelines, monitoring, and lifecycle management on AWS Bedrock, Azure AI, and Google Vertex AI.
- **Enterprise integration:** Connecting AI systems to existing ERP, CRM, and data platforms across complex multi-vendor environments.
- **Consulting and architecture:** Agentic system design reviews, AI strategy, and build-vs-buy assessment for large technology programmes.
- **Staff augmentation:** Dedicated AI engineers embedded in client teams, a common engagement model for EPAM's enterprise clients.

## What are EPAM Systems' key strengths in AI agent development?

EPAM's primary strength is delivery scale and enterprise trust. Based on publicly available case studies and annual report disclosures:

- **Scale of talent:** Over 50,000 engineers globally, with dedicated AI/ML practices across multiple geographies. Large programmes can be staffed quickly.
- **Cloud partnership depth:** EPAM holds partner status with AWS, Azure, and GCP — the three dominant cloud providers — giving access to early tooling, support channels, and co-sell relationships.
- **Regulated industry experience:** Published case studies reference work with major financial institutions and healthcare systems where data handling, audit trails, and compliance documentation are mandatory.
- **Long client relationships:** EPAM's revenue concentration in a small number of large clients (disclosed in SEC filings) suggests deep, sustained engagements rather than one-off project delivery.

## How much does EPAM Systems charge for AI agent development?

EPAM does not publish a rate card. Pricing is T&M (time and materials) or retainer for most engagements, with rates aligned to large-firm enterprise consulting. Publicly available data points — including Glassdoor salary ranges and industry benchmarks — suggest blended billing rates are at the higher end of the IT services market. EPAM is not typically competitive on price for projects under $250k.

| Engagement model | How it works | Typical fit |

| Dedicated team | Allocated engineers managed by EPAM | Large, sustained AI engineering programmes |

## What types of AI agents does EPAM Systems build?

Based on published case studies and service documentation:

- **Financial services automation agents:** Risk analysis, compliance document review, and transaction monitoring agents for banking and insurance clients.
- **Content intelligence agents:** Media asset classification, summarisation, and multi-modal processing pipelines.
- **Enterprise knowledge agents:** Internal knowledge retrieval systems using RAG on proprietary document corpora.
- **Process automation agents:** LLM-driven replacements for rule-based RPA workflows in manufacturing and logistics.

## What should you consider before hiring EPAM Systems for AI agent development?

The primary consideration is fit: EPAM is built for enterprise clients and operates accordingly.

- **Minimum viable engagement size:** EPAM is not typically set up to run small, fast proof-of-concept engagements. Projects under ~$200k–$250k are unlikely to be prioritised.
- **Process overhead:** Enterprise governance, SOW negotiation, and onboarding timelines mean EPAM is not the fastest route to a working prototype.
- **Delivery geography:** Much of EPAM's engineering capacity is in Eastern Europe (Ukraine, Poland, Hungary, and neighbouring countries). Geopolitical risk should be factored into long-term programme planning.
- **Rate verification:** EPAM rates are not public. Ensure you receive a detailed breakdown of blended versus senior/junior rates during the scoping process.

## Who is EPAM Systems best suited for?

EPAM is the strongest fit for organisations that:

- Are building AI agent systems within complex, regulated enterprise environments (finance, healthcare, insurance).
- Need large teams quickly and have existing relationships with AWS, Azure, or GCP that EPAM can extend.
- Have established vendor management and procurement processes suited to large IT services contracts.
- Require documented compliance, audit trails, and security controls as part of delivery.

It is less likely to be the right fit for startups, early-stage product teams, or any engagement where speed-to-prototype and cost efficiency outweigh scale and governance.
