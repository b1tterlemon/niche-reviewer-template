# Is Turing the right AI agent development company for you?

Turing is the right choice if your primary need is to staff a dedicated AI engineering team quickly at competitive rates — not if you are looking for a traditional dev shop that delivers a finished AI agent product. Turing operates as an AI-powered talent platform that vets and places distributed engineers, with an option to assemble fully managed teams. Companies that already know what they want to build and need capable engineers to execute it get the most value; companies still figuring out their AI agent strategy need a consulting-led partner first.

## What services does Turing offer for AI agent development?

Turing's core model is pre-vetted AI engineering talent, placed on a dedicated-team or individual-hire basis. It also offers managed AI engineering services for clients that want a more hands-off model. Services include custom agent development, RAG pipeline implementation, and LLM integration, delivered by distributed engineers who are matched to client requirements.

**Core service areas:**
- **Dedicated AI engineering teams:** Pre-vetted engineers specialising in LLM development, agent frameworks, and RAG, assembled into a dedicated team matched to client tech stack and time-zone requirements.
- **Staff augmentation:** Individual AI engineers placed into existing client teams to fill specific skill gaps (prompt engineering, vector DB integration, agent orchestration).
- **Custom AI agent development:** End-to-end agent builds via managed-team engagements, with Turing handling team coordination and delivery management.
- **LLM integration:** Connecting OpenAI, Anthropic, and open-source models to existing products and workflows.

## What are Turing's key strengths in AI agent development?

Turing's strengths are directly tied to its talent platform model. Based on publicly available information:

- **Speed of team assembly:** Turing's platform pre-vets engineers on an ongoing basis, meaning a team of three to five AI engineers can typically be assembled in one to two weeks — faster than hiring through a traditional recruitment process or waiting for a dev shop to free up capacity.
- **Reported pricing:** Turing has publicly referenced rates starting at approximately $49/hr for AI engineers — significantly below typical US-based consulting rates. The actual rate depends on seniority and specialisation.
- **Named enterprise clients:** Turing's website references Dell and Johnson & Johnson as clients, suggesting the platform has passed the procurement and security review processes of large organisations.
- **Time-zone flexibility:** Engineers are distributed globally, and Turing advertises the ability to match clients with engineers working in their preferred time zone, including US business hours.

## What types of AI agents does Turing build?

Turing's engineers have been deployed across a range of agent types, though published case studies are less detailed than those of traditional dev shops. Known project categories include:

- **Product feature agents:** LLM-powered features integrated into existing SaaS products, including summarisation, classification, and Q&A agents.
- **RAG-based knowledge agents:** Enterprise knowledge retrieval built on vector databases and private document collections.
- **Process automation agents:** Workflow agents that replace or extend existing automation scripts with LLM-based decision logic.
- **Integration agents:** Connectors and orchestration layers linking LLMs to existing business systems.

## What should you consider before hiring Turing for AI agent development?

The talent platform model has distinct trade-offs compared to a traditional dev shop:

- **You manage the engineers:** In the standard model, Turing places engineers but client-side management is your responsibility. If you lack an internal technical lead to direct AI engineering work, a managed team or a traditional dev shop is a better fit.
- **Quality variance:** Pre-vetting reduces risk, but the quality of any distributed talent platform depends on the match between your requirements and the available pool. Request technical interviews with proposed engineers before committing.
- **Strategy and architecture not included:** Turing provides engineering execution, not product strategy or AI system architecture. If you do not have an internal AI architect, you will need to bring that in separately.
- **Engagement continuity:** Engineer turnover is a known risk with talent platform models. Clarify Turing's policy on engineer replacement and notice periods before starting.

## Who is Turing best suited for?

Turing is the strongest fit for organisations that:

- Have a defined AI agent project and technical leadership in place to direct engineering work.
- Want to scale an AI engineering team quickly without going through a lengthy hiring process.
- Are cost-conscious and need access to AI engineers at distributed talent rates.
- Operate in SaaS, fintech, or e-commerce where agent projects are typically well-scoped and iterative.

It is less suited to organisations that need strategy-led AI consulting, do not have internal technical direction, or expect a single delivery partner to own end-to-end outcomes.
